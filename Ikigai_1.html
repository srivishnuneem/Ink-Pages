"Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life" by Héctor García and Francesc Miralles is divided into several sections that explore the concept of ikigai and provide insights on how to apply its principles to find purpose and happiness in life. Here's a detailed breakdown of the book's contents:

1 Introduction: Discovering Ikigai

In the introduction of "Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life," authors Héctor García and Francesc Miralles set the stage for their exploration of the concept of ikigai and its significance in Japanese culture. They introduce readers to the concept's multi-faceted nature and its potential to contribute to a fulfilling and meaningful life. Here's a detailed breakdown of this introductory section:

1.1 Setting the Scene

The authors begin by recounting their journey to the village of Ogimi in Okinawa, Japan, which is known for its high number of centenarians and reputation as a "village of longevity." This serves as the starting point for their exploration of the principles of ikigai.

1.2 Defining Ikigai

The term "ikigai" is introduced as a Japanese concept that signifies "a reason for being" or "a reason to wake up in the morning." The authors emphasize that ikigai is not just a philosophical idea, but a practical approach to living a fulfilling life.

1.3 The Four Pillars

García and Miralles introduce the four pillars of ikigai, which are identified as the convergence of four fundamental elements: what you love, what you are good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. These pillars form the foundation for discovering one's ikigai.

1.4 Exploring the Overlapping Circles

The authors depict the four pillars as overlapping circles, with the intersection at the center representing one's ikigai. This visual representation helps readers understand how various aspects of their lives can align to create a sense of purpose.

1.5 Ikigai and Longevity

The authors point out that the village of Ogimi provides a real-world example of how the principles of ikigai contribute to longevity and happiness. They share anecdotes of the villagers' daily lives and their strong sense of purpose, even in old age.

1.6 The Universality of Ikigai

While rooted in Japanese culture, the authors suggest that the principles of ikigai are universal and can be applied to anyone, regardless of their cultural background.

1.7 Purpose and Fulfillment

García and Miralles convey that the quest for ikigai is essentially a quest for purpose and fulfillment. They emphasize that discovering one's ikigai is an ongoing journey and that the pursuit itself can lead to a richer and more rewarding life.

1.8 A Guide to the Book

The authors conclude the introduction by previewing the book's chapters and the journey readers will embark upon to explore the depth and applications of the ikigai philosophy.

In essence, the introduction sets the tone for the book's exploration of ikigai, providing context, definitions, and initial insights into how this concept can contribute to a more meaningful and joyful existence.
